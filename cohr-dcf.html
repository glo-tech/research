Coherent Corp.
The Quiet One

Everyone is talking about Lumentum. Lumentum is up 1,255% in a year. Coherent does the same thing, for the same customers, using largely the same technology. It's up 449%. That gap is the whole thesis.

I found Coherent by looking for whatever Lumentum's less famous cousin was. Turned out to be a $7B revenue company with a $2 billion check from Nvidia on its balance sheet and capacity booked through 2028. I had to read the earnings call twice to make sure I wasn't missing something. I wasn't.

The Business

Light, not electrons. That's the whole thing.

When you train an AI model or run inference at scale, you need GPUs talking to each other constantly. For a long time they did this over copper wires. Copper works fine until you need to move more data than copper can carry, which is exactly what's happening right now. The solution is to use light instead of electricity. Optical transceivers convert electrical signals into light pulses, send them through fiber, and convert them back. Coherent makes those components.

The reason this matters for AI: a single Nvidia GPU cluster at scale has thousands of connections. As models get bigger and inference demand grows, those connections need to carry more bandwidth. The shift from 400G to 800G to 1.6T transceivers is happening in real time, and Coherent is one of maybe three companies in the world that can actually make 1.6T transceivers at volume. The other two are Lumentum and Applied Optoelectronics.

Coherent also makes the chips that go inside those transceivers. Indium phosphide lasers. They're scaling their 6-inch InP fabrication capacity and plan to more than quadruple it by end of 2027. That manufacturing capability is why Nvidia wrote them a $2 billion check. Not a customer contract. An equity investment. That's a different level of commitment.

I genuinely didn't understand what an optical transceiver was before writing this. The short version: it's a plugin module that slots into a network switch and makes the connection. The 1.6T part means it can move 1.6 terabits of data per second. For context, that's roughly 200 gigabytes per second. The reason AI needs this is that GPUs sit in racks and have to talk to each other constantly during training. Faster connections mean less time waiting.
COHR vs LITE

Same demand. Very different valuations.

This is the core of the pitch. Lumentum has been the AI optics story of the year. It's up 1,255% in twelve months. The press is all over it. Meanwhile Coherent is doing essentially the same thing, growing just as fast on the datacenter business, and trading at one-third the earnings multiple.

The gap exists for a few reasons. Coherent has a legacy industrial segment that's dragging on overall growth -- industrial revenue fell 41% YoY in Q3 while datacenter grew 41%. The blended number looks less impressive. Second, Coherent was late to fully pivot its messaging to AI. Lumentum's CEO is better at the investor story. Third, Coherent is a bigger company with more debt, which makes the valuation math look uglier on a per-share basis even when the underlying business is strong.

None of those things change what the datacenter business is actually doing.

Why I think this works
Nvidia's $2B equity stake is not normal. Customers don't take equity positions in their suppliers. Strategic partners do. That relationship gives Coherent visibility and manufacturing support no contract can replicate.

Capacity booked through 2028. Some contracts extend to 2030. In a world where hyperscaler capex cycles can reverse, multi-year take-or-pay agreements are genuinely valuable.

The valuation gap vs LITE is hard to justify on fundamentals. 49x vs 159x earnings for businesses doing the same thing for the same customers is a big gap to maintain indefinitely.
What could go wrong
Industrial segment is still 25% of revenue and shrinking. It's a drag on margins and narrative. If it doesn't stabilize, it keeps clouding the AI story.

CPO (co-packaged optics) could disrupt the transceiver model. If hyperscalers move to CPO faster than expected, pluggable transceiver volumes could soften. Both Coherent and Lumentum are betting on CPO too, but the timeline is uncertain.

Customer concentration. Nvidia is a massive portion of the revenue outlook. Jensen Huang's comments move this stock. That's a single point of risk.
